Hello, all! Have you heard the song LEASE from the My Merry May OST? It's a great example of early 2000s Japanese visual novel music.

It's composed by Takeshi Abo, who's also worked on various other Japanese visual novels. The song has a very nostalgic and familiar feeling even outside of contemporary Japanese music, so much so that it's become related to a certain visual aesthetic called 'Frutiger Aero'.

But, it's odd to me how this *specific* song was picked out as a definer of that visual aesthetic, considering there's many other songs that sound just similar. This kind of music actually reminds me of the jazzy music some companies will play when you're on hold. And that music is worldwide... interesting, isn't it?

Still, the roots of this song are still connected heavily to contemporary Japanese music, which further more comes down to Western jazz. From the perspective of music theory, the song isn't actually doing anything very special besides a good amount of modal interchange within the chords and usage of secondary dominants.

What gives this kind of music its 'moe' (萌え) feeling is the instrumentation choices used. So much of the music of the early 2000s in Japan was created using synthesizers from Korg, Roland, Yamaha, and others. These synthesizers came with preset patches, which were used constantly throughout differents genres including visual novel OSTs. This is likely why music of this genre, or moreso music of this time period made in Japan has been so situated with the 'Frutiger Aero' aesthetic.

If you don't know, Frutiger Aero revolves around early 2000s design patterns used on computers and in promotional artwork and technology. The auditory equivalent of that aesthetic is early 2000s musical synthesizers, and because of their consistency with preset patches, its easy to use their similarity across songs to define the aesthetic as a whole.

What you're hearing in the song LEASE is not necessarily some great, fancy music theory techniques (even though it does have some nifty cliches); you're hearing very familiar retro sounds from those Japanese synthesizers. You automatically connect that to early 2000s technology, which in turn relates to 'Frutiger Aero'!

In a way, I think a lot of what I'm rambling about goes without saying, but it's something that interested me. I was really just wondering what made LEASE sound so oddly familiar on my first listen, and then I just connected it to sounding like many other of those early visual novel OSTs that used the same ensemble and instrumentation.

Anyway, thank you for reading my thoughts! I appreciate you coming here. I'll write again soon!
